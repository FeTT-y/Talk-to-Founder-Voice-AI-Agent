## Positioning

Maneuver is a small, senior product studio. We help companies design and ship AI-native products — voice agents, agentic workflows, and AI features inside existing applications. We take a small number of engagements at a time and we don't pitch to RFPs.

The one-line version we give people: *"We build the thing other people only prototype."*

We're founder-led, opinionated, and selective. The teams that get the most out of working with us are early-stage startups that need senior judgment fast, and mid-market companies that have an AI initiative stuck in pilot and need someone to actually ship it.

## Services

Five core offerings. Most engagements start with Discovery and roll into one of the others.

### 1. AI product strategy
Two-week sprint to figure out what to build, how to scope it, and what *not* to build. Output: a product spec, a risk register, and a wireframe-grade prototype where it helps. Best for teams that have "we should be doing AI" pressure but don't yet have a sharp bet.

### 2. Voice AI agents
End-to-end voice agent builds — discovery, scheduling, support, sales-qualification, intake. We handle the whole stack: design, the realtime pipeline, evals, ops, and telephony integration if needed.

### 3. AI features inside existing products
Drop-in RAG, agentic workflows, and copilots inside existing SaaS apps. We embed with your engineering team and ship in-tree — not over the wall.

### 4. Prototype sprints
One-week sprints to ship a working demo you can put in front of investors, customers, or a board. Real code, not Figma. Used most often by founders who need to derisk a thesis before raising.

### 5. Fractional CTO / advisory
For early-stage teams that need product-engineering judgment without a full-time hire. Usually a four-month minimum.

## Process

Four phases. We say no to projects that won't fit this shape.

1. **Discovery** — Free 30-minute intro call (this one), then a paid one-week deep-dive. We define the problem, the success metric, and the single constraint most likely to kill the project. End with a written go/no-go.
2. **Design** — Two-week design sprint. We build the smallest thing that proves the bet. End with a working prototype plus a build spec.
3. **Build** — Four to eight weeks. Engineering and design ship the production version with your team in the loop the whole way.
4. **Handoff** — Documentation, a one-week pairing with your team, and 90 days of retained on-call so you can catch issues with us still on the line.

## Selected work

### Case study — agentic onboarding for an HR-tech Series A
Replaced an eight-step manual onboarding flow with a single AI conversation. The new flow handles edge cases that previously required a human CSM, escalates cleanly when it can't, and integrated into their existing Postgres schema without a migration. Result: 65% drop in time-to-activation and 2.3x lift in week-one retention. Six weeks end-to-end.

### Case study — voice intake agent for tele-mental-health
Patient intake calls were averaging 22 minutes with a human coordinator and bottlenecking the platform's ability to schedule clinicians. We built a voice intake agent that runs the standard intake in eight minutes, defers anything clinically nuanced to a human, and writes a structured note into the EHR. Live with twelve thousand patient calls processed in the first quarter.
